5 Mistakes Every New Pitmaster Makes

Everyone screws up their first few cooks. Don’t be that guy who pulls the ribs early or keeps lifting the lid every five minutes. Here’s what to avoid:

  • 🔥 Don’t skip preheating — your smoker needs time.
  • 🔥 Stop checking the meat every 10 minutes — trust the process.
  • 🔥 Use a thermometer — guessing doesn’t work.
  • 🔥 Don’t overload the grill — learn one meat at a time.
  • 🔥 Clean your gear — it ain’t “seasoning” if it’s old grease.

What I Learned Cooking for 140 Sailors a Day

On a submarine, there’s no room for excuses. You make food fast, hot, and right. That work ethic? It built ChowdownBBQ. Whether you're feeding a crew or just your own, prep is everything — don’t show up to your own cookout unprepared.

How to Handle Heat Like a Pro

Good BBQ isn’t about sauce. It’s about heat control. Direct vs. indirect. Hot zone vs. cool zone. Start with a 2-zone fire: sear on one side, finish slow on the other. Use wood chunks, not chips. Let the smoke do the work.
